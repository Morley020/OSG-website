About the Founder

Brigadier General Oliver Mubita (Ret.)

A distinguished Army General, visionary strategist, and internationally recognised defence and security expert with vast experience in Visionary and Transformational Leadership — at home and abroad.

Gen. Ollie is not only a practitioner but brings to the fore a hybrid of academic and practical experience. He has served with the United Nations, African Union, and Regional Mechanisms like the Southern African Development Community (SADC) — bridging the gap between Public, Private, and Civil Society sectors. Crucially, 28 of his 30 years in service have been dedicated to Leader Development and Education locally and beyond, notably overseeing the capacity development of the SADC Standby Force domiciled across 16 member countries.

Academically, Gen. Mubita holds a Master of Military Arts and Sciences in Strategic Studies from the prestigious US Army War College (in collaboration with Central Michigan University), a Bachelor's in Business Administration in Project Management, and is currently pursuing an MBA in Leadership and Strategy. He was also trained at the Cadet School of the Irish Military College, the Canadian Peace Support Training Centre, and the African Union Commission.

Through Ollie's Strategic Group, he employs a number of tested change models whilst cultivating and promoting operational resilience and recovery — incorporating the interplay between systems to achieve desired effects. It is an opportunity of renewal, affording a chance to build and enhance systems. Let us do it together!
